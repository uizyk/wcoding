student life

student life

Alongside their academic exploration, the students at SPPS participate in diverse clubs and student organizations. Steeped in the principles of independency and responsibility, student organizations are completely student-led, thus creating a community that thinks spontaneously, moves ambitiously and plays responsibly. More than 22 clubs plan numerous school-wide events each school year and work closely with the faculty and staffs to host meaningful activities. In learning how to balance academic pursuits and extracurricular interests, students develop various organizational skills that are critical to their social development. The school's intimate size facilitates student ownership and therefore deepens their commitments.

community service

community service

SPPS is motivated by the belief that nurturing students to develop a clear sense of responsibility will develop global citizens who transform the world for the better, to achieve overall harmony. Understanding our nature and the need for interpersonal relationships we strongly encourage students to participate in as many community service activities as possible to increase their awareness for social responsibility.

School-Sponsored Community Service Activities

"Shoes of Hope" helping child labors in developing countries
"Daoom English Library Tutoring" for kids from low income families
"Constructing Shelters for victims from natural disasters" to Korea and Philippines

support for student life

The SPPS Homeroom system provides a strong network of support to make students feel connected and engaged while providing opportunities in leadership. Accomplishing various homeroom projects and competitions cooperatively throughout the year, each homeroom builds a unique identity that enhances a stronger sense of cohesion and belongingness to the community.
